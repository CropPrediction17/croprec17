Mungbeans

The global mungbean area is about 7.3 million ha and global output is about 5.3 million tons with India and Myanmar each supplying about 30% of this, China 16%, and Indonesia 5%.

In India, mungbean is grown on an area of about 3 million hectares with the production of about 1 million tonnes. The major mungbean grown states are Orissa, Maharashtra, Andhra Pradesh, Telangana, Rajasthan, Madhya Pradesh, Bihar, Karnataka, and Uttar Pradesh. Mung beans do best on fertile, sandy loam soils with good internal drain- age and a pH in the range of 6,3 and 7. Mung beans require slightly acid soil for best growth.If they are grown in. rotation,lime to attain pH of the most acid sensitive crop. Root growth can be restricted on heavy clays. Mungbean grows in a wide range of climatic conditions. A warm humid climate with temperature ranging from 25⁰ C to 35⁰ C, with 400-550 mm rainfall, well distributed during the growing period of 60 - 90 days, is suitable for cultivation. The mungbean crop should be fertilized with phosphorus and potassium at the level of 100-100 PK kg ha‾1 for achieving satisfactory seed yield.
